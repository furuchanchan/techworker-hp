Why Now Why act now

OpenAI and Anthropic alike.
The very companies that built AI are now moving into adoption support.

About 95% of enterprise generative-AI initiatives stall without ever producing results.Source: MIT, 2025

No one understands this reality better than the model developers themselves. In 2026, OpenAI is hiring hundreds of deployment consultants and has formed major partnerships with the likes of McKinsey, BCG, and Accenture. Anthropic, too, has partnered with leading consulting firms and is rapidly building out a large-scale partner network.

Why are the very creators of AI investing so heavily in "adoption support"? The answer is simple. The tools are already more than capable enough.

What separates success from failure is not the performance of the AI.
It is the adoption design that feeds your business context into AI.

The Problem Challenges

Does any of this sound familiar?

You signed up for ChatGPT or Copilot, but only a handful of employees actually use it.
The PoC is complete — yet there is no clear path to company-wide rollout.
Leadership is championing AI, but teams on the ground don't know where to start.
The tools are deployed, but working hours haven't gone down.
If even one of these applies, there's room for context engineering.

In most cases, the reason AI fails to deliver is not the tool. The root cause is that your organization's operational knowledge, decision criteria, and decision-making processes have never been put into a form AI can use. We call solving this "context engineering."

Our Approach Our strengths

Context engineering is
the foundation of the AI era.

Operations manuals, tacit knowledge, decision criteria, internal terminology, approval workflows — these are all "context." People share them naturally with one another, but you cannot hand them to AI as-is. We are a team of specialists who shape that context into a form AI can understand and put to work.
